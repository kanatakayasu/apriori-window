\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We introduced SC-DenseAttrib, the first method to apply the Synthetic Control Method to pattern support time series for causal attribution of support changes to external events.
Our framework formalizes four key concepts---Donor Pool, Counterfactual Support Trajectory, Causal Effect on Support, and Item-Disjoint Pattern Control---and provides theoretical guarantees under parallel trends.

Experiments demonstrated that SC-DenseAttrib:
(1) accurately recovers causal effects with mean absolute error below 0.04;
(2) controls the false positive rate at 0\% under the null;
(3) handles seasonal confounds via the synthetic control construction; and
(4) provides interpretable counterfactual trajectories that simple permutation tests cannot offer.

The primary limitation is the dependence on donor pool size for statistical power: at least 20 item-disjoint donors are needed for $p < 0.05$.
This requirement may be challenging in datasets with few items or highly correlated pattern supports.

Future work includes:
(1) extending to multiple simultaneous interventions via augmented SCM;
(2) relaxing the item-disjointness criterion using conditional independence tests;
(3) integrating with the dense interval detection pipeline for fully automated causal attribution; and
(4) application to real-world retail and healthcare datasets.
