Rare Dense Patterns

Mining Locally Dense but Globally Rare Itemsets

Target: KDD 2026 / SDM 2026

Abstract

Frequent itemset mining algorithms rely on anti-monotonicity to prune the search space, but this property systematically eliminates globally rare patterns. We observe that many important patterns are globally rare yet locally dense: they co-occur infrequently overall but exhibit concentrated bursts within narrow temporal windows. We formalize this as the Rare Dense Pattern (RDP) mining problem and prove a Weak Anti-Monotonicity property that enables Apriori-style pruning. Our Two-Phase Mining algorithm is provably sound and complete, achieving 100% recovery with 80%+ false positive reduction and sub-second scalability to 100K transactions.

Key Concepts

Rare Dense Pattern

An itemset that is globally rare (support < max threshold) but has at least one locally dense interval where co-occurrence count meets a minimum threshold within a sliding window.

Weak Anti-Monotonicity

The local density condition is anti-monotone (subsets of dense patterns are also dense), enabling Apriori-style pruning even though the full RDP condition is neither monotone nor anti-monotone.

Two-Phase Mining

Phase 1: Sliding-window Apriori discovers all locally dense patterns. Phase 2: Global rarity filter retains only rare patterns. Provably sound and complete.

Motivation

Flash Sales

Luxury item bundles purchased by only 0.1% of customers overall, but 30% of daily transactions during a 2-week sale.

Security Incidents

Attack signatures (port scan + credential stuffing + lateral movement) in <0.01% of logs, but clustered within minutes during an active attack.

Seasonal Patterns

Product combinations (sunscreen + insect repellent + camping gear) with low annual support but weekly spikes during summer.
