Anti-Dense Intervals and Contrast Dense Patterns:
Symmetric Extensions of Dense Interval Mining

DSAA 2026 / ICDM 2026

Abstract

Dense interval mining identifies contiguous time periods where a pattern's sliding-window support exceeds a threshold. However, the existing framework is asymmetric: it characterizes pattern presence but not absence. We propose two symmetric extensions: Anti-Dense Intervals (maximal periods of sustained low support) and Contrast Dense Patterns (a framework for comparing dense interval structures across temporal regimes). We define a complete taxonomy of topology changes, prove reverse monotonicity for efficient mining, and provide permutation-based statistical tests with FDR control.

Key Contributions

  • Anti-Dense Intervals — Formalize the dual of dense intervals with a reverse monotonicity property enabling top-down search. Same O(n log n) complexity as dense interval detection.
  • Contrast Dense Patterns — Compare dense interval structures across two temporal regimes with a complete taxonomy of four topology change types.
  • Statistical Testing — Permutation test for structural change significance with Benjamini-Hochberg FDR correction.
  • Experimental Validation — Five experiments on synthetic and simulated retail data demonstrating practical effectiveness.
