\title{High-Utility Dense Intervals:\\Joint Frequency-Utility Temporal Mining}

\author{\IEEEauthorblockN{Anonymous Author(s)}
\IEEEauthorblockA{Under Review}}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
Frequent itemset mining and high-utility itemset mining address complementary aspects of transactional data analysis: the former identifies commonly co-occurring items, while the latter captures economically significant item combinations. However, neither framework is designed to detect \emph{temporal} concentrations---time intervals where specific itemsets exhibit both high frequency and high utility. We introduce the \emph{Utility-Dense Interval} (UDI) framework, which extends sliding-window dense interval detection with a utility dimension. We define Window Utility (WU) and Window Transaction Weighted Utilization (WTWU), prove that WTWU provides an anti-monotone upper bound for window-level utility pruning, and present an Apriori-style algorithm that jointly enforces frequency and utility thresholds. Experiments on synthetic and semi-synthetic datasets demonstrate that (i) the algorithm recovers injected high-utility dense patterns with 100\% precision across multiple seeds, (ii) TWU-based pruning reduces candidate evaluation, and (iii) runtime scales near-linearly with transaction count. Our framework fills a gap at the intersection of temporal pattern mining and utility-aware analysis.
\end{abstract}
